About

My name is Juliana Vargas and I am a photography student at San Jose State University. When I was 12, my dad gave me my first camera. That was when my passion for photography started. I grew up in Costa Rica and I was lucky enough to grow up in a house with a very large garden which meant that I had my own little world to explore. I enjoyed being able to capture what was around me in my own way. Growing up as a very shy person, I really liked being able to communicate with others through my images without needing to talk.

Once I graduated high school, I was a hundred percent sure that I wanted to study photography. I moved back to Los Angeles, CA less than a month after I graduated to pursue my photographic studies. Through my courses, I started to really enjoy taking still life photography. I started to merge the use of color and compositing to create visually interesting still life photos. The use of color, as a main component of my photos, and compositing are still present throughout my work, even in those that aren’t still life photos.

Some of the concepts I come up with for my works have a message I am trying to transmit to the viewer, but not all of them do. Some of my works focus on themes that I think aren’t addressed as much as they should be and/or themes that relate to my life. Sometimes, however, I just want to create visually intriguing and engaging images. Either way, whether there is a message or there isn’t, I want my work to make the viewers find themselves in a place between familiarity and discomfort. Ultimately, I wish to inspire others to explore and expand their own way of viewing the world.
